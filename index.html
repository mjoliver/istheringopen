Why This Exists

You know how it goes — the track's been closed for an cleanup, and now it's a guessing game. You're sat in the car with one bar of signal, trying to predict exactly when the barriers will lift. Get the timing right, and you're first out on a clear track; get it wrong, and you're buried in the access queue, destined for a lap full of traffic.

This site was built by a motorsport fan — for exactly that moment. One question, answered instantly. It fetches live data directly from the Nürburgring's own API, caches it aggressively on your device, and serves everything from a CDN close to the Eifel — so it loads fast even when the network around the track is packed with a few thousand other people doing the same thing.

It's a free, community-run project with no ads and no tracking. Just the answer.

🔋 Smart Data Refresh

To save battery and bandwidth, checking frequency depends on track activity:

  • Live Track 30s
  • Scheduled Today 10m
  • Standby mode Up to 24h

⚡ Optimised for the Track

  • ~20 KB total — entire app shell, first load, on the wire
  • Zero external requests — no third-party fonts, trackers, or CDN calls
  • Cached on your device after first visit — reloads instantly, works offline
  • Single upstream API call shared across all users via server-side cache

⚠️ Data comes from the official Nürburgring API. The track can close at any time due to weather or incidents — always confirm before making a long drive.
